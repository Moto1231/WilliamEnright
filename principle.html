There is one question that matters.

Is it good for the business?

Technology has no value on its own. Its value is measured by the competitive advantage it creates, the problems it solves, and the opportunities it enables.

Everything else follows from there.

The Enright Principles

1. Business First. Technology Second.

Technology is a tool, not the objective. The right solution begins with understanding the business, its challenges, its opportunities, and its goals.

2. Technology Should Strengthen Competitive Advantage.

Standardization, automation, and modernization only matter when they make the business stronger. Technology should preserve what makes a company unique, not force it to become identical to everyone else.

3. Understand Before Recommending.

The best technology decisions come from understanding the people, processes, and objectives behind the request. The solution should follow the problem—not the other way around.

4. Advice Means Nothing Without Execution.

Strategy matters. Execution matters more. The best recommendations are those that can move from concept to reality.
